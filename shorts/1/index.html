Unbricking a BIOS-bricked motherboard

Has that ever been in a situation when flashing a new BIOS goes wrong and your motherboard become a useless brick? We've all been there, so keep on reading.

How to flash a new BIOS without the motherboard even POSTing

Note: This tutorial is meant for AMI BIOSes. Said that, you can google the procedure for your particular BIOS.
  1. Download a BIOS for your motherboard from the vendor website
  2. If it's an EXE or ZIP try to extract the files within with something like 7-Zip. The BIOS file should be:
    • Usually ending with .ROM
    • Being of a power of 2 size (like 1MB, 2MB, 4MB, ...)
  3. If your motherboard is old enough to have a floppy connector go head and plug that old crap
  4. If (lucky you) your motherboard doesn't have any floppy connector it means that we can just use a normal USB key
  5. Find a floppy, or if you are lucky a USB key and format them using FAT
  6. Take the BIOS file, rename it to AMIBOOT.ROM and copy it to the floppy/USB key
    • If your motherboard is by HP use instead HPBIOS.ROM
  7. Insert the floppy/USB key in the offending computer
  8. If your motherboard has a PS/2 keyboard connector please one it, otherwise just use your normal USB keyboard
  9. While powering on your PC press and hold CTRL+HOME until you see the floppy drive/USB key LED blinking
  10. Wait until you hear 5 beeps
  11. Reboot
